Backflush

After the filtering cycle stops, the process enters a backflush mode, which cleans the cake from the filters.

Air is blown at a low pressure into the top of the filter column which forces clean dielectric through the filters from the centre of the mesh cylinders and backflushes the caked debris from the filter material. A short time period is allowed for this to happen. The cake is gradually dislodged and then falls to the bottom of the filter column.

The filter and back-flush cycles alternate until the feed tank is empty. After approximately 20 back-flush cycles have occurred Cake discharge is carried out.

NOTE: The current number of back flushes carried out is shown on the Backflush Counter located on the lower Control panel below the mimic panel.

The number of backflushes / cycles before a discharge is performed can be programmed as desired.

If running in AUTO mode, at the end of this stage, the cycle will switch to either filter or discharge.
