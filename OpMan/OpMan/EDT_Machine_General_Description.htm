EDT Machine General Description

An EDT machine takes a ground roll, usually made of steel, with a relatively smooth shiny finish and creates a ‘texture’ on its surface that gives it a much more ‘matt’ or opaque finish. The purpose of this is to harden the roll and thus extend its durability.

EDT stands for Electro-Discharge Texturing.

The process uses a series of electrodes submerged in dielectric oil and held close to the surface of the roll to generate highly controlled sparks. These sparks then create a series of tiny craters (surface texture) on the surface of the roll.

The sparks have an approximate voltage of 300V DC (on an open circuit) and deliver a current of 1-62 Amps at a frequency of up to 100 KHz.
